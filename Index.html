Chicken Briyani

Making

  • Mix chicken with yogurt, turmeric, chili powder, biryani masala, lemon juice, salt, and ginger-garlic paste. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (longer for better flavor).
  • Boil water in a large pot. Add salt, whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon). Add soaked basmati rice and cook till 70–80% done. Drain and set aside.
  • Heat oil/ghee in a large pan. Fry sliced onions until golden brown; set aside half for garnish. Add green chilies, ginger-garlic paste; sauté till raw smell goes. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft. Add marinated chicken and cook until almost done (gravy should be semi-thick). Add chopped coriander and mint
  • In a heavy-bottom pot, spread a layer of chicken mixture. Add a layer of partially cooked rice. Repeat layering if needed. Sprinkle fried onions, saffron milk (optional), mint, coriander, and garam masala on top
  • Cover the pot tightly with a lid or seal with dough. Place on low flame for 20–25 minutes (or use a hot iron tawa beneath the pot). Let it rest for 10 minutes before opening.
  • Gently mix before serving to combine layers. Serve hot with raita, salad, or boiled egg.

Chole Curry

Making

  1. Soak 1 cup chickpeas overnight. Drain and pressure cook with 3 cups water, salt, and tea bags (optional) for 4–6 whistles or until soft. Discard tea bags and set chickpeas aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and bay leaf. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili; sauté till raw smell goes.
  3. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and chole masala. Stir and cook for 30 seconds. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates from the masala.
  4. Add boiled chickpeas to the masala. Mix well and add some water to adjust consistency. Simmer for 15–20 minutes to blend flavors.
  5. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with bhature, puri, rice, or roti.
